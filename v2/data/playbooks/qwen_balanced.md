# Qwen's Balanced Investment Playbook

## 1. Core Strategy
- **Balanced Approach**: The core strategy is to balance between value, growth, and momentum. This approach aims to capture both undervalued and high-growth opportunities while also capitalizing on short-term price movements.
- **Value**: Focus on companies with strong fundamentals that are undervalued relative to their intrinsic worth.
- **Growth**: Invest in companies with strong earnings growth, high return on equity, and a history of innovation.
- **Momentum**: Identify stocks with positive price momentum and strong technical indicators.

## 2. Asset Selection
- **Stocks**: Prioritize US equities with a price of at least $5. Avoid penny stocks and highly speculative companies.
- **Sectors**:
  - **Technology**: High-growth potential and innovation.
  - **Healthcare**: Defensive sector with consistent demand.
  - **Consumer Staples**: Stable and less cyclical.
  - **Financials**: Value opportunities and economic sensitivity.
  - **Industrials**: Cyclical but with strong growth potential.
- **ETFs**: Use ETFs for diversification and to gain exposure to specific sectors or themes. Examples include:
  - Technology (e.g., XLK)
  - Healthcare (e.g., XLV)
  - Consumer Staples (e.g., XLP)
  - Financials (e.g., XLF)
  - Industrials (e.g., XLI)

## 3. Trade Horizon
- **Typical Holding Period**: 1 to 3 months. This allows enough time to capture medium-term trends while maintaining flexibility to adjust the portfolio as needed.
- **Short-Term Trades**: Hold for 1 to 2 weeks if driven by momentum or short-term catalysts.
- **Long-Term Holdings**: Hold for up to 6 months for high-quality, long-term growth stocks.

## 4. Risk Profile
- **Stop-Loss**: Set a Stop-Loss at 10% below the entry price. This helps to limit losses and protect capital.
- **Take-Profit**: Target a Take-Profit level at 15% to 20% above the entry price. This provides a balanced approach to capturing gains while avoiding excessive risk.
- **Position Sizing**: 
  - **Initial Position Size**: Start with a 10% to 15% allocation per trade.
  - **Max Position Size**: Increase to 25% for high-confidence trades.
  - **Max Open Positions**: Maintain a maximum of 15 open positions to ensure diversification and manage risk.

## 5. Data Usage
- **Fundamentals**:
  - **Valuation Metrics**: P/E, P/B, P/S, EV/EBITDA.
  - **Financial Health**: Debt-to-Equity, Current Ratio, Quick Ratio.
  - **Growth Metrics**: Revenue Growth, Earnings Growth, ROE.
- **Technicals**:
  - **Trend Indicators**: Moving Averages (50-day, 200-day).
  - **Momentum Indicators**: RSI, MACD.
  - **Volume Analysis**: Relative Volume, On-Balance Volume (OBV).
- **Macroeconomic Data (FRED)**:
  - **Interest Rates**: Fed Funds Rate, 10-Year Treasury Yield.
  - **Inflation**: CPI, PPI.
  - **Economic Indicators**: GDP, Unemployment Rate.
- **News and Sentiment (FINRA News)**:
  - **Company-Specific News**: Earnings reports, management changes, product launches.
  - **Sector and Market News**: Regulatory changes, industry trends, macroeconomic events.

By adhering to this playbook, the goal is to achieve consistent returns with controlled drawdowns, balancing risk and reward in a disciplined manner.
