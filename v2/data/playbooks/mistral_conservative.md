# **CONSERVATIVE PORTFOLIO MANAGEMENT PLAYBOOK**
*Capital Preservation First | Large-Cap & ETF Focus | Low Volatility | Sortino > 1.0*

---

## **1. Core Strategy**
**Primary Approach:** *Value-Oriented, Macro-Aware, Low-Volatility Growth*
- **Value Bias:** Prefer established, large-cap companies with strong balance sheets, consistent cash flows, and attractive valuations (low P/E, P/B, and debt-to-equity ratios).
- **Macro Overlay:** Monitor interest rates, inflation, and fiscal policy (via FRED) to adjust sector exposure. Reduce risk in late-cycle or high-volatility regimes.
- **Momentum Guardrails:** Avoid "value traps" by requiring improving fundamentals (e.g., rising ROE, stable margins) before entry.
- **ETF Core:** Allocate ≥50% of capital to broad-market or sector-specific ETFs (e.g., SPY, QQQ, XLK) for diversification and lower drawdowns.

**Avoid:**
- High-growth, unprofitable companies (e.g., speculative tech, biotech).
- Leveraged or inverse ETFs.
- Illiquid small-caps (<$1B market cap).

---

## **2. Asset Selection**
### **Stock Picks (≤50% of Portfolio)**
**Criteria:**
- **Fundamentals (Primary):**
  - Market Cap: ≥$10B (large-cap).
  - Valuation: P/E < 20, P/B < 3, EV/EBITDA < 12.
  - Profitability: ROE ≥ 10%, net margin ≥ 10%.
  - Dividend: Yield ≥ 2% (optional but preferred).
  - Debt: Debt/Equity < 0.5, interest coverage > 5x.
- **Technicals (Secondary):**
  - Price ≥ 50-day SMA (avoid downtrends).
  - RSI(14) < 70 (avoid overbought).
  - Volume: ≥500K avg. daily volume.
- **Sectors (Prioritized):**
  1. **Defensive:** Healthcare (XLV), Consumer Staples (XLP), Utilities (XLU).
  2. **Cyclical (Selectively):** Financials (XLF) in rising-rate environments, Energy (XLE) if oil >$70/bbl.
  3. **Tech (Blue-Chip Only):** Microsoft (MSFT), Apple (AAPL), Nvidia (NVDA) *only if* valuations are reasonable.

**Screening Process:**
1. **Initial Filter:** Use fundamental screens (e.g., Finviz) to identify large-cap value stocks.
2. **Macro Check:** Cross-reference with FRED data (e.g., 10Y Treasury yield, CPI) to confirm sector alignment.
3. **News Scan:** FINRA news for red flags (e.g., lawsuits, earnings misses).
4. **Technical Confirmation:** Ensure price is above key moving averages (50/200-day SMA).

### **ETF Allocation (≥50% of Portfolio)**
**Core Holdings (60% of ETF Allocation):**
- **SPY** (S&P 500): 30% of ETF capital.
- **QQQ** (Nasdaq-100): 20% of ETF capital (tech overweight).
- **VTI** (Total Market): 10% of ETF capital (diversification).

**Tactical ETFs (40% of ETF Allocation):**
- **Sector ETFs:** Rotate into XLV (Healthcare), XLP (Staples), or XLU (Utilities) during market stress.
- **Bond ETFs (if needed):** TLT (Long-Term Treasuries) or BND (Total Bond Market) if recession signals emerge (e.g., inverted yield curve).

---

## **3. Trade Horizon**
- **Typical Hold Period:** 3–12 months.
  - **ETFs:** Buy-and-hold unless macro conditions deteriorate (e.g., Fed hikes, recession signals).
  - **Stocks:** Hold until fundamentals deteriorate (e.g., earnings miss, debt spike) or technicals break (e.g., close below 200-day SMA).
- **Exceptions:**
  - **Short-Term (1–3 months):** If a stock/ETF rallies >15% in <3 months, take partial profits (50% of position).
  - **Long-Term (12+ months):** Only for core ETFs (SPY, QQQ) or stocks with durable competitive advantages.

---

## **4. Risk Management**
### **Position Sizing**
- **Individual Stocks:** 5–10% of portfolio.
- **ETFs:** 10–20% of portfolio (larger allocations for core holdings like SPY).
- **Max Open Positions:** 10 (to avoid over-diversification).

### **Stop-Loss Rules**
- **Initial Stop-Loss:** 10–15% below entry price (wider for volatile stocks, tighter for ETFs).
  - *Example:* For a stock at $100, stop-loss at $85–$90.
- **Trailing Stop-Loss:** After a 10% gain, trail stop to 5% below recent high.
- **Macro Stop-Loss:** If S&P 500 drops >10% from recent high, reduce equity exposure by 30% (shift to cash or TLT).

### **Take-Profit Rules**
- **Partial Profit-Taking:** Sell 50% of position at +15% gain.
- **Full Exit:** Sell remaining 50% at +25% gain or if fundamentals deteriorate.
- **ETF Exits:** Hold unless macro conditions change (e.g., Fed pivot, recession).

### **Drawdown Limits**
- **Portfolio-Level:** If drawdown exceeds 10%, reduce equity exposure by 20% (shift to cash or TLT).
- **Single-Position:** If any position drops >20%, exit immediately (regardless of stop-loss).

---

## **5. Data & Tool Prioritization**
| **Tool**       | **Priority** | **Use Case**                                                                 |
|----------------|-------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Fundamentals** | ★★★★★       | Screen for value (P/E, ROE, debt), confirm earnings stability.              |
| **FRED Macro**   | ★★★★☆       | Monitor interest rates (10Y Treasury), inflation (CPI), and GDP growth.     |
| **Technicals**   | ★★★☆☆       | Confirm trends (SMA, RSI), set stop-losses, and avoid overbought stocks.    |
| **FINRA News**   | ★★☆☆☆       | Check for red flags (e.g., lawsuits, management changes) before entry.      |

**Daily Workflow:**
1. **Macro Check (FRED):** Review 10Y Treasury yield, CPI, and leading indicators (e.g., PMI).
2. **Fundamental Screen:** Update watchlist for stocks/ETFs meeting value criteria.
3. **Technical Review:** Ensure no positions are in downtrends (price < 50-day SMA).
4. **News Scan (FINRA):** Flag any negative headlines for open positions.
5. **Rebalance:** Adjust ETF allocations if macro conditions shift (e.g., reduce QQQ if rates rise).

---

## **6. Performance Targets**
- **Return:** 8–12% annualized (lower than aggressive strategies, but with lower drawdowns).
- **Drawdown:** ≤15% max (target <10%).
- **Sortino Ratio:** >1.0 (prioritize downside risk-adjusted returns).
- **Win Rate:** ≥60% (favor high-probability, low-risk trades).

---

## **7. Prohibited Actions**
- **No leverage or margin.**
- **No short-selling or inverse ETFs.**
- **No options, crypto, or penny stocks.**
- **No day trading (T+1 constraint).**
- **No concentration in single stocks (>10% of portfolio).**
- **No holding through earnings announcements (exit 2 days prior, re-enter if no gap down).**
