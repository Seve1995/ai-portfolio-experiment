# Qwen's Aggressive Portfolio Management Playbook

## 1. Core Strategy
- **Momentum and Catalyst-Driven**: Focus on stocks with strong momentum and upcoming catalysts. This strategy aims to capitalize on short-term price movements and event-driven opportunities.
- **High-Conviction Ideas**: Concentrate on the highest-conviction ideas, taking larger position sizes (15-25%) to maximize returns.

## 2. Asset Selection
- **Stocks and ETFs**: Prioritize US equities (price >= $5) and ETFs that align with the momentum and catalyst-driven strategy.
- **Sectors**:
  - **Technology**: High-growth sectors with frequent product launches and innovation.
  - **Healthcare**: Biotech and pharmaceutical companies with upcoming clinical trial results or regulatory approvals.
  - **Consumer Discretionary**: Companies with new product launches, strong brand recognition, and positive consumer trends.
  - **Industrials**: Firms with significant contract wins, project completions, or technological advancements.
- **ETFs**: Use sector-specific ETFs to gain exposure to broader market trends and diversify risk while maintaining a focus on momentum and catalysts.

## 3. Trade Horizon
- **Short to Medium-Term**: Hold positions for 1-3 months, depending on the strength of the momentum and the timing of the catalysts. Quick exits if the momentum fades or the catalyst does not materialize as expected.

## 4. Risk Profile
- **Stop-Loss %**: 10% from the entry price. Tighter stop-losses to cut losses fast and preserve capital.
- **Take-Profit %**: 20-30% from the entry price. Aim for high returns but be prepared to take profits if the stock shows signs of weakening momentum or if the catalyst has been fully priced in.

## 5. Data Usage
- **Technical Analysis**: Prioritize technical indicators such as moving averages, RSI, MACD, and volume trends to identify momentum and potential entry/exit points.
- **Fundamentals**: Use fundamental data to validate the underlying strength of the company, including earnings growth, revenue growth, and analyst ratings.
- **News and Catalysts**: Leverage FINRA news and other financial news sources to stay informed about upcoming catalysts, such as earnings reports, product launches, and regulatory decisions.
- **Macroeconomic Data (FRED)**: Use macroeconomic data to understand the broader economic environment and its impact on specific sectors and stocks. However, this will be a secondary consideration compared to technical and fundamental analysis.

## Summary
- **Core Strategy**: Momentum and Catalyst-Driven
- **Asset Selection**: US Equities (price >= $5) and ETFs, focusing on Technology, Healthcare, Consumer Discretionary, and Industrials.
- **Trade Horizon**: 1-3 months
- **Risk Profile**: 10% Stop-Loss, 20-30% Take-Profit
- **Data Usage**: Prioritize Technical Analysis, Fundamentals, and News/Catalysts, with secondary consideration to Macroeconomic Data.

This playbook is designed to aggressively pursue alpha by concentrating on high-conviction ideas, leveraging momentum, and capitalizing on catalysts, while maintaining disciplined risk management.
