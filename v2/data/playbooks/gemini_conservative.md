# Conservative Capital Preservation Playbook

This playbook outlines the meta-strategy for a conservative portfolio manager, prioritizing capital preservation, stable returns, and a robust Sortino ratio above 1.0. The focus will be on minimizing drawdowns and generating consistent, albeit potentially lower, returns over the long term.

## 1. Core Strategy

The core strategy is primarily **Value-driven** with a significant **Macro-economic overlay**.

*   **Value Investing:** We will seek out established, high-quality companies trading below their intrinsic value. This involves a deep dive into fundamentals, balance sheet strength, consistent profitability, and sustainable competitive advantages (economic moats). We are not chasing growth at any cost but rather looking for durable businesses that can weather various economic cycles.
*   **Macro-economic Analysis:** A thorough understanding of the broader economic environment (interest rates, inflation, GDP growth, employment, geopolitical risks) will inform our overall market exposure, sector allocation, and risk posture. We will adjust our defensive positioning based on macro indicators, increasing caution during periods of economic uncertainty or potential downturns.

## 2. Asset Selection

Asset selection will focus on stability, quality, and diversification.

*   **Stocks:**
    *   **Focus:** Large-cap, blue-chip companies with a proven track record of profitability, strong balance sheets, consistent free cash flow generation, and a history of dividend payments. We prioritize companies with wide economic moats, stable business models, and experienced management teams.
    *   **Sectors:** Favor defensive sectors such as Consumer Staples, Utilities, and Healthcare due to their inelastic demand and resilience during economic downturns. Industrials and established, profitable Technology giants (e.g., mature software, hardware leaders) may be considered if valuations are attractive and their business models are robust. We will generally avoid highly cyclical, speculative, or commodity-dependent sectors unless a compelling, long-term value proposition is identified with significant downside protection.
    *   **Exclusions:** No penny stocks, no crypto, no options. Avoid highly leveraged companies or those with unproven business models.
*   **ETFs:**
    *   **Core Holdings:** Broad-market equity ETFs (e.g., S&P 500, total market) will form a significant portion of the portfolio to ensure broad diversification, reduce idiosyncratic risk, and capture overall market returns with lower volatility.
    *   **Targeted Exposure:** Sector-specific ETFs may be used to gain diversified exposure to favored defensive sectors or to tactically reduce exposure to overvalued segments of the market.
*   **Position Sizing:** Individual positions will typically range from **5% to 10%** of the total capital. This conservative sizing ensures diversification and limits the impact of any single underperforming asset on the overall portfolio. The maximum number of open positions will be 15.

## 3. Trade Horizon

Our trade horizon will be **medium to long-term**, typically ranging from several weeks to several months, and potentially longer.

*   We are investors, not traders. Positions will be held as long as the fundamental investment thesis remains intact, the company continues to meet our quality criteria, and the valuation remains reasonable.
*   Frequent trading will be avoided to minimize transaction costs and reduce exposure to short-term market noise. Exits will primarily be driven by a deterioration of fundamentals, significant overvaluation, or a material shift in the macro-economic outlook that invalidates the original investment thesis.

## 4. Risk Profile

Capital preservation is the paramount objective. Our risk management strategy is designed to minimize drawdowns and protect capital.

*   **Stop-Loss:** A mandatory stop-loss will be set at **12% to 15%** below the entry price. This wider range is chosen specifically to avoid being stopped out by normal market volatility and allows for the natural fluctuations of high-quality, long-term holdings. The exact percentage within this range will be determined by the specific asset's volatility and underlying support levels.
*   **Take-Profit:** We do not employ fixed, aggressive take-profit targets. Instead, profit-taking will be dynamic, triggered by:
    *   Significant overvaluation of the asset based on fundamental analysis.
    *   A material deterioration of the company's fundamentals or competitive position.
    *   A significant shift in the macro-economic environment that warrants a reduction in overall market exposure or a rotation out of a specific sector.
    *   While not a fixed percentage, we aim for a favorable risk-reward profile, typically seeking potential upside of 1.5x to 2x our stop-loss percentage (e.g., 18-30% potential upside) *if* the fundamental valuation supports it. However, we are content with consistent, moderate gains and will prioritize protecting accumulated profits.
*   **Sortino Ratio:** Our primary performance metric will be the Sortino Ratio. We aim to consistently achieve a Sortino Ratio above 1.0, indicating that we are generating returns efficiently relative to downside risk.

## 5. Data Usage

Our data usage will prioritize fundamental and macro-economic analysis.

1.  **Fundamentals (Highest Priority):** This is the bedrock of our strategy. We will meticulously analyze financial statements (income statements, balance sheets, cash flow statements), competitive landscapes, management quality, industry trends, and intrinsic valuation models (e.g., DCF, multiples). This informs our initial asset selection and ongoing thesis validation.
2.  **FRED Macro (High Priority):** Macro-economic data from FRED will be continuously monitored to assess the overall economic health, inflation trends, interest rate environment, employment figures, and other leading indicators. This data will guide our overall portfolio allocation, sector weighting, and risk posture.
3.  **FINRA News (Medium Priority):** News feeds will be used to stay abreast of company-specific developments (earnings reports, M&A, regulatory changes), industry news, and broader market sentiment. This information serves as a critical input for risk assessment and for validating or re-evaluating existing investment theses.
4.  **Technicals (Lowest Priority):** Basic technical analysis (e.g., long-term moving averages, support/resistance levels) may be used as a secondary tool to inform optimal entry and exit points *after* a fundamental decision has been made. We will use it to avoid buying into sharp downtrends or selling into sharp uptrends, but technical indicators will not be the primary driver for initiating or closing positions.

This playbook ensures a disciplined, conservative approach focused on long-term capital preservation and stable growth, aligning with our core objective of minimizing downside risk.
