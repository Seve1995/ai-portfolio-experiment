# Investment Playbook: DeepSeek (Conservative)

## 1. Core Strategy
**Capital Preservation-Focused Value with Quality Momentum**

- **Primary Objective**: Capital preservation through lower volatility and controlled drawdowns
- **Secondary Objective**: Steady, risk-adjusted returns exceeding cash equivalents
- **Approach**: Blend of quality-focused value (established companies trading below intrinsic value) with momentum confirmation (positive price trends)
- **Risk-Adjusted Focus**: Target Sortino ratio > 1.0, maximum drawdown < 15%
- **Market Regime Adaptation**: Reduce position sizes and increase cash during high volatility periods

## 2. Asset Selection

### Primary Universe
- **Large-Cap Focus**: Minimum market cap $10B, preference for S&P 500 constituents
- **Liquidity Requirement**: Minimum average daily volume > 1M shares
- **Price Floor**: Strict adherence to $5+ requirement, preference for $20+
- **Quality Filters**: Companies with consistent profitability (5+ years), strong balance sheets (debt/equity < 1.0), and sustainable competitive advantages

### Sector Preferences
- **Defensive Sectors**: Healthcare, Consumer Staples, Utilities (30-40% allocation)
- **Cyclical Quality**: Technology (established players only), Industrials (20-30% allocation)
- **Income-Oriented**: Real Estate (REITs with strong occupancy), Energy (integrated majors only)
- **Avoid**: Highly speculative sectors, unprofitable growth stories, extreme cyclicality

### ETF Usage Strategy
- **Core Holdings**: 40-60% portfolio allocation to broad-market ETFs
  - SPY (S&P 500) or IVV: 20-30% core exposure
  - Sector ETFs for targeted exposure: XLV (Healthcare), XLP (Consumer Staples)
  - Quality factor ETFs: QUAL, SPHQ
- **Tactical ETFs**: For sector rotation or defensive positioning
  - Defensive: XLU, VDC
  - Low volatility: USMV, SPLV

### Selection Process
1. **Screen for Quality**: Profitability, financial strength, management quality
2. **Valuation Check**: P/E below sector average, reasonable PEG ratios
3. **Momentum Confirmation**: Positive 3-6 month price trend, above 200-day MA
4. **Risk Assessment**: Beta < 1.2, lower volatility than market

## 3. Trade Horizon

### Position Categories
- **Strategic Core (40-60%)**: 6-12+ month horizon
  - Broad-market ETFs and highest-quality individual stocks
  - Only exited on fundamental deterioration or extreme overvaluation
- **Tactical Positions (30-50%)**: 3-9 month horizon
  - Individual stocks and sector ETFs
  - Exited when thesis plays out or better opportunities emerge
- **Opportunistic (0-10%)**: 1-3 month horizon
  - Mean-reversion plays or special situations
  - Strict size limits and tighter risk controls

### Portfolio Turnover Target
- Annual turnover: 50-80% (deliberate, low-churn approach)
- Avoid overtrading; let winners run with trailing stops

## 4. Risk Management

### Position Sizing
- **Typical Position**: 5-10% of portfolio
- **Maximum Position**: 15% (only for highest-conviction core holdings)
- **Minimum Position**: 5% (avoid dilution of effort)
- **Cash Buffer**: Maintain 10-20% cash for opportunities and risk management

### Stop-Loss Strategy
- **Initial Stop-Loss**: 15-20% below entry (wider to avoid normal volatility)
- **Trailing Stops**: Implement after 10%+ gain, trail at 10-15%
- **Stop Placement**: Based on technical levels (support/resistance) not arbitrary percentages
- **Sector-Specific Stops**:
  - Stable sectors (Staples, Utilities): 15% stops
  - Moderate volatility (Healthcare, Industrials): 18% stops
  - Higher volatility (Tech, Discretionary): 20% stops

### Take-Profit Strategy
- **Primary Method**: Trailing stops rather than fixed profit targets
- **Partial Profit Taking**: Trim 25-50% of position after 20-30% gain
- **Rebalance Triggers**: When position grows to >15% of portfolio

### Portfolio-Level Risk Controls
- **Maximum Drawdown Alert**: Reduce risk if portfolio down >8%
- **Sector Concentration**: Max 25% in any single sector
- **Single Stock Limit**: Max 15% in any individual security
- **Correlation Management**: Avoid highly correlated positions

## 5. Data Usage Priority

### Primary Tools (Daily)
1. **Fundamentals Screening**: Quality, valuation, financial strength metrics
2. **Technical Analysis**: Price trends, support/resistance, relative strength
3. **Market Breadth**: Advance/decline, new highs/lows for context

### Secondary Tools (Weekly)
1. **FRED Macro Data**: 
   - Yield curve (10Y-2Y spread)
   - Inflation indicators (CPI, PCE)
   - Unemployment trends
   - Use for defensive positioning, not market timing
2. **FINRA News & Sentiment**: 
   - Earnings surprises and guidance changes
   - Major corporate actions
   - Sector rotation signals
3. **Economic Calendar**: FOMC meetings, major economic releases

### Decision Framework
1. **Top-Down Context**: Macro environment assessment (defensive/neutral/aggressive stance)
2. **Bottom-Up Selection**: Individual security analysis meeting quality criteria
3. **Technical Timing**: Entry/exit based on price action and volume
4. **Risk Check**: Position sizing and stop placement

### Data Integration Rules
- Never trade on single data point
- Require confluence of 2+ tools for entry decisions
- Fundamentals determine what to buy, technicals determine when
- Macro data informs portfolio beta, not individual stock selection

## Daily Operating Principles

1. **Preservation First**: Every decision evaluated through risk lens first
2. **Patience Over Action**: Wait for high-probability setups; cash is a position
3. **Process Over Outcome**: Follow the playbook consistently
4. **Adapt Gradually**: Adjust to changing markets through position sizing, not strategy abandonment
5. **Document Rationale**: Clear thesis for every position entered

*This playbook represents a disciplined, risk-aware approach designed to compete effectively in the 6-month experiment while staying true to conservative portfolio management principles.*
