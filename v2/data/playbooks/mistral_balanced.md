# **Mistral Balanced Portfolio: Investment Playbook**
*6-Month Autonomous Trading Experiment – Core Principles & Execution Framework*

---

## **1. Core Strategy**
**Hybrid Balanced Approach** – A disciplined blend of **growth, value, and moderate momentum**, with macro-awareness to navigate sector rotations and economic cycles.

- **Primary Allocation (60-70%)**:
  - **Growth at a Reasonable Price (GARP)**: Focus on companies with sustainable earnings growth (10-25% YoY), strong competitive moats, and reasonable valuations (PEG < 1.5). Prioritize secular trends (AI, cloud, healthcare innovation, energy transition).
  - **Quality Value**: Undervalued stocks (P/E < 15, P/B < 2) with improving fundamentals (rising ROE, FCF yield > 5%), temporary headwinds, or turnaround potential. Avoid "value traps" via rigorous cash flow analysis.

- **Secondary Allocation (20-30%)**:
  - **Moderate Momentum**: Stocks with strong relative strength (top 20% of 6-month price performance) *and* improving fundamentals (e.g., earnings revisions, institutional accumulation). Exit if momentum fades (e.g., 50-day MA crossover).
  - **Macro Hedges**: Tactical ETF positions (e.g., gold, long-duration Treasuries, or inverse ETFs) during high-uncertainty regimes (recession signals, Fed pivot expectations).

- **Tertiary Allocation (10-20%)**:
  - **Dividend Growth**: High-quality dividend growers (5+ years of increases, payout ratio < 60%, yield 2-4%) for income and downside protection. Focus on sectors with pricing power (utilities, consumer staples, healthcare).

**Avoid**:
- Pure momentum (high drawdown risk).
- Deep value without catalysts (illiquidity, permanent impairment risk).
- Overconcentration in speculative themes (e.g., meme stocks, unprofitable "story" stocks).

---

## **2. Asset Selection**
### **Stock Picking Framework**
**Top-Down + Bottom-Up Hybrid**:
1. **Macro Filter (Top-Down)**:
   - Use **FRED** to monitor:
     - Yield curve (inversion = defensive tilt).
     - Inflation (PCE > 3% = favor energy, materials; < 2% = growth sectors).
     - Unemployment (rising = consumer discretionary underweight).
   - **FINRA News** for:
     - Sector-specific catalysts (e.g., FDA approvals, regulatory changes).
     - Insider transactions (clustered buying/selling by executives).

2. **Fundamental Screen (Bottom-Up)**:
   - **Growth**:
     - Revenue/EPS growth > 15% YoY (3-year CAGR).
     - Gross margin stability (> 40%).
     - ROIC > WACC (sustainable competitive advantage).
   - **Value**:
     - EV/EBITDA < 10, P/FCF < 15.
     - Positive FCF and net debt/EBITDA < 3.
     - Piotroski F-Score > 6 (financial health).
   - **Dividend**:
     - Dividend growth rate > 5% YoY (5-year history).
     - Free cash flow coverage > 1.5x.

3. **Technical Confirmation**:
   - **Entry**: Price above 200-day MA (for growth/value) or 50-day MA (for momentum).
   - **Avoid**: Stocks in confirmed downtrends (lower highs/lows) or with high short interest (> 20%).

### **Sector Allocation**
**Dynamic but Constrained**:
- **Core Holdings (60-70%)**:
  - **Technology** (20-25%): Semiconductors, cloud, cybersecurity (GARP focus).
  - **Healthcare** (15-20%): Biotech (catalyst-driven), medtech, pharma (dividend growers).
  - **Industrials** (10-15%): Aerospace, automation, defense (long-term capex trends).
- **Cyclical/Tactical (20-30%)**:
  - **Consumer Discretionary** (5-10%): Luxury, e-commerce (momentum-driven).
  - **Energy/Materials** (5-10%): Only during inflationary regimes or supply shocks.
- **Defensive (10-20%)**:
  - **Consumer Staples** (5-10%): Dividend growers with pricing power.
  - **Utilities** (5%): Regulated utilities with yield > 3%.

**ETF Usage**:
- **Core ETFs (10-20% of portfolio)**:
  - **SPY/QQQ**: For broad market exposure (only during confirmed uptrends).
  - **Sector ETFs**: XLC (comm services), XLV (healthcare), XLE (energy) for tactical tilts.
- **Hedging ETFs (0-10%)**:
  - **GLD/IAU**: Gold during Fed pivot expectations or geopolitical risk.
  - **TLT**: Long-duration Treasuries during recession signals.
  - **SQQQ**: Inverse Nasdaq (max 5% allocation, short-term hedge).

## **3. Trade Horizon**
| **Strategy**       | **Typical Hold Period** | **Max Hold (No Catalyst)** | **Exit Conditions**                          |
|--------------------|-------------------------|----------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| GARP Growth        | 3-9 months              | 12 months                 | PEG > 2, growth deceleration, 20% drawdown. |
| Quality Value      | 6-12 months             | 18 months                 | Fair value reached, fundamentals deteriorate.|
| Momentum           | 1-3 months              | 6 months                  | 50-day MA crossover, relative strength fade.|
| Dividend Growth    | 12+ months              | Indefinite (if healthy)   | Payout ratio > 80%, dividend cut.           |
| Macro Hedges       | 1-6 months              | 6 months                  | Regime change (e.g., Fed hikes resume).     |

**Exceptions**:
- **Earnings Catalysts**: Hold through earnings if pre-event momentum is strong; exit if miss.
- **Black Swan Events**: Liquidate hedges immediately if crisis abates (e.g., VIX > 40).

---

## **4. Risk Management**
### **Position Sizing**
- **Single Stocks**: 5-15% of portfolio (max 15 positions).
  - **10-15%**: High-conviction GARP/value names with strong fundamentals.
  - **5-10%**: Momentum or speculative growth (higher risk).
- **ETFs**: 5-20% (max 25% for core ETFs like SPY).
- **Hedges**: 5-10% (e.g., GLD, TLT).

### **Stop-Loss Rules**
| **Strategy**       | **Stop-Loss %** | **Trailing Stop?** | **Take-Profit %** |
|--------------------|-----------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| GARP Growth        | 10-12%          | Yes (8% trailing)  | 25-30%            |
| Quality Value      | 8-10%           | No                 | 20-25%            |
| Momentum           | 7-9%            | Yes (5% trailing)  | 15-20%            |
| Dividend Growth    | 15%             | No                 | 15-20%            |
| Macro Hedges       | 5-7%            | No                 | 10-15%            |

**Additional Rules**:
- **Hard Stop**: Mandatory exit if portfolio drawdown reaches **12%** (liquidate weakest 2 positions).
- **Correlation Check**: No more than 30% of portfolio in highly correlated stocks (e.g., 3 semiconductor stocks).
- **Liquidity**: Avoid stocks with average volume < 500K shares/day.

## **5. Data & Tool Prioritization**
### **Primary Tools (Daily/Weekly)**
1. **Fundamentals (Morningstar, Yahoo Finance)**:
   - Screen for GARP/value metrics (PEG, P/FCF, ROIC).
   - Monitor earnings revisions (upward = bullish).
2. **Technicals (TradingView)**:
   - 50/200-day MA crossovers, RSI (avoid >70/<30), volume spikes.
   - Relative strength vs. sector/benchmark.
3. **Macro (FRED)**:
   - Yield curve, inflation (PCE), unemployment, ISM PMI.
   - Fed policy signals (dot plot, Powell speeches).
4. **News (FINRA, Benzinga)**:
   - Earnings pre-announcements, FDA decisions, M&A.
   - Insider transactions (clustered buying = bullish).

### **Secondary Tools (Weekly/Monthly)**
- **Sentiment (AAII, Put/Call Ratio)**: Contrarian signals (extreme bearishness = buy).
- **Sector Rotation (XLY/XLP ratio)**: Consumer discretionary vs. staples.
- **Seasonality (Stock Trader’s Almanac)**: Avoid "Sell in May" if macro supports.

### **Avoid**
- Overfitting to backtests.
- Chasing "hot" stocks without fundamentals.
- Ignoring macro regime shifts (e.g., inflation spikes).

---

## **6. Portfolio Construction Rules**
1. **Diversification**:
   - Max 25% in any one sector.
   - Max 15% in any single stock.
   - At least 3 sectors represented at all times.
2. **Rebalancing**:
   - **Monthly**: Trim winners >15% of portfolio; add to laggards if fundamentals intact.
   - **Quarterly**: Full review of all positions (exit if thesis broken).
3. **Cash Buffer**:
   - Maintain 5-10% cash for opportunistic buys during drawdowns.
4. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Hold winners >1 year where possible (long-term capital gains).
   - Harvest losses to offset gains (if applicable).

---

## **7. Drawdown Protocol**
- **10% Drawdown**: Review all positions; exit weakest 1-2 holdings.
- **12% Drawdown**: Liquidate bottom 20% of performers (by return).
- **15% Drawdown**: Move to 50% cash; reassess macro regime.

---

## **8. Performance Benchmarks**
- **Primary**: S&P 500 (SPY) – Target **alpha of 3-5% annualized**.
- **Secondary**:
  - **Risk-Adjusted**: Sharpe ratio > 1.0.
  - **Drawdown**: Max <15% (target <10%).
  - **Win Rate**: >55% of trades profitable.

This playbook ensures **consistent, risk-controlled returns** while adapting to market regimes. Adherence is mandatory.
