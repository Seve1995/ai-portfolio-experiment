# Conservative Portfolio Management Playbook

## 1. Core Strategy
- **Strategy Type:** Value and Macro-Driven
- **Objective:** Prioritize capital preservation with a focus on stable, established companies and broad-market ETFs. Aim for steady, modest returns with minimal drawdowns.

## 2. Asset Selection
- **Stock Selection Criteria:**
  - Focus on large-cap companies with a strong track record of profitability and stable earnings.
  - Preference for companies with a history of dividend payments.
  - Avoid high-volatility stocks and sectors.
- **Sector Preferences:**
  - Favor defensive sectors such as Consumer Staples, Utilities, and Healthcare.
  - Consider Financials and Industrials for diversification.
- **ETFs:**
  - Utilize broad-market ETFs (e.g., S&P 500, Dow Jones Industrial Average) for diversified exposure.
  - Consider sector-specific ETFs for targeted exposure to preferred sectors.

## 3. Trade Horizon
- **Holding Period:** 
  - Typical position held for 3 to 6 months, aligning with the experiment duration.
  - Flexibility to adjust based on market conditions and individual stock performance.

## 4. Risk Profile
- **Position Sizing:**
  - Allocate 5% to 10% of capital per trade to maintain diversification and manage risk.
- **Stop-Loss Strategy:**
  - Set wider stop-losses at approximately 15% below the entry price to avoid being stopped out by normal market volatility.
- **Take-Profit Strategy:**
  - Target a conservative take-profit level of 20% to 25% above the entry price.
- **Sortino Ratio Target:**
  - Aim for a Sortino ratio above 1.0 to ensure returns are achieved with minimal downside risk.

## 5. Data Usage
- **Fundamentals:**
  - Prioritize fundamental analysis to assess company financial health, focusing on earnings stability, dividend history, and valuation metrics.
- **Macro Data:**
  - Utilize FRED macroeconomic data to understand broader economic trends and their potential impact on sectors and asset classes.
- **Technicals:**
  - Use technical analysis sparingly, primarily to identify entry and exit points within the context of broader fundamental and macroeconomic analysis.
- **News and Sentiment:**
  - Monitor FINRA news for any significant developments that could impact the portfolio, but avoid overreacting to short-term news events.

## Summary
This playbook is designed to guide a conservative investment approach, emphasizing capital preservation and steady growth through diversified exposure to large-cap stocks and ETFs. By focusing on value and macroeconomic factors, the strategy aims to achieve a balance between risk and return, suitable for the constraints and objectives of the 6-month trading experiment.
