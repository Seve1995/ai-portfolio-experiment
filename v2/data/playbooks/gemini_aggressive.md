# Investment Playbook: Aggressive Alpha Seeker

This playbook outlines my core strategy for maximizing returns over the 6-month trading experiment. My objective is to generate significant alpha against the benchmark, accepting higher drawdowns in pursuit of outsized gains. I will concentrate capital on my highest-conviction ideas, leveraging momentum and catalysts, while employing strict risk management to cut losses swiftly.

## 1. Core Strategy: Momentum & Catalyst-Driven Growth

My primary strategy is a high-conviction, aggressive approach focused on **Momentum and Catalyst-Driven Growth**.

*   **Momentum:** I will identify and capitalize on stocks exhibiting strong price and volume momentum, indicating robust institutional interest and a clear uptrend. I seek companies that are outperforming their sector and the broader market.
*   **Catalysts:** I will actively seek out companies with identifiable, near-term catalysts that can drive significant price appreciation. These include, but are not limited to, strong earnings reports, product launches, regulatory approvals, M&A activity, strategic partnerships, or significant analyst upgrades.
*   **Alpha Generation:** The goal is to achieve a high Information Ratio by concentrating capital on a select few, high-potential opportunities. I am prepared to accept higher volatility and potential drawdowns in pursuit of superior returns.

## 2. Asset Selection: High-Growth Equities & Thematic ETFs

My universe is US Equities (price >= $5) and ETFs.

*   **Stock Selection:**
    *   **Growth Focus:** Prioritize companies demonstrating strong revenue and earnings growth, innovative products/services, and clear market leadership or disruptive potential within their industries.
    *   **Relative Strength:** Select stocks exhibiting superior relative strength compared to their peers and the overall market.
    *   **Technical Breakouts:** Look for stocks breaking out of established consolidation patterns on significant volume, signaling the start of a new uptrend.
    *   **Liquidity:** Focus on liquid stocks to ensure efficient entry and exit.
*   **Sector Favoritism:** I will primarily target high-growth sectors such as Technology (AI, cloud computing, cybersecurity), Biotechnology (novel drug development, medical devices), Renewable Energy, and disruptive Consumer Trends. However, I remain sector-agnostic and will pursue strong momentum/catalyst plays wherever they emerge.
*   **ETFs:** ETFs will be used sparingly, primarily for broad market hedges during periods of high uncertainty or to gain exposure to a strong thematic trend if individual stock opportunities are limited or too risky. My preference is for concentrated individual stock positions for maximum alpha potential.

## 3. Trade Horizon: Short to Medium-Term (Catalyst-Dependent)

My typical trade horizon will be **short to medium-term**, generally ranging from a few weeks to 3 months.

*   **Dynamic Holding:** Positions will be held as long as the underlying momentum persists, the catalyst narrative remains intact, and the stock continues to meet my performance criteria.
*   **Agile Exits:** I will not hesitate to exit positions quickly if momentum breaks, the catalyst fails to materialize as expected, or if the stock hits its stop-loss. I prioritize capital preservation and redeployment into new, higher-conviction opportunities.

## 4. Risk Profile: Aggressive Position Sizing with Tight Stops

My risk profile is aggressive, balancing concentrated positions with strict loss cutting.

*   **Position Sizing:** I will allocate **15% to 25%** of capital per trade for my highest-conviction ideas. This concentration is key to generating significant alpha. I will typically hold 4-7 open positions, well within the 15-position limit, to maintain focus.
*   **Stop-Loss (%):** A **mandatory 6-8% stop-loss** from the entry price will be enforced on every trade. This is non-negotiable. I will also utilize technical stop-losses (e.g., below a key moving average or significant support level) if they provide an even tighter exit point. Cutting losses fast is paramount.
*   **Take-Profit (%):**
    *   **Initial Target:** My initial take-profit target will be **15-25%** gain, aiming for a minimum 2:1 to 3:1 risk/reward ratio.
    *   **Dynamic Management:** I will not be rigid with take-profit targets. If a stock continues to exhibit strong momentum and positive catalysts, I will let winners run. In such cases, I will implement a trailing stop-loss to lock in gains while allowing for further appreciation. I will re-evaluate positions upon reaching the initial target and consider scaling out or tightening stops to protect profits.

## 5. Data Usage: Technicals & News First

My data usage will prioritize tools that support my momentum and catalyst-driven strategy.

1.  **Technicals (High Priority):**
    *   **Focus:** Price action, volume analysis, relative strength (RSI), moving averages (10-day, 20-day, 50-day, 200-day EMAs/SMAs), MACD.
    *   **Application:** Essential for identifying entry/exit points, confirming momentum, detecting breakouts, and setting precise stop-loss levels.
2.  **FINRA News (High Priority):**
    *   **Focus:** Breaking news, earnings reports, analyst upgrades/downgrades, M&A rumors, product announcements, regulatory updates, insider activity.
    *   **Application:** Critical for identifying immediate catalysts, understanding market sentiment shifts, and validating the narrative behind a stock's momentum.
3.  **Fundamentals (Medium Priority):**
    *   **Focus:** Revenue growth, EPS growth, profit margins, market share, competitive landscape, balance sheet health (debt levels).
    *   **Application:** Used to validate the underlying quality and growth potential of companies identified through technicals and news, ensuring the momentum is fundamentally sound and not purely speculative.
4.  **FRED Macro (Low Priority):**
    *   **Focus:** Interest rates, inflation, GDP growth, employment data.
    *   **Application:** Provides a broader economic context and helps assess overall market risk appetite, informing my general market exposure and position sizing, but not directly used for individual stock selection.
