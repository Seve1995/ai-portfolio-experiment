# Investment Playbook: Balanced Growth & Value Strategy

As a Balanced Portfolio Manager, my objective is to achieve consistent, risk-adjusted returns by strategically blending growth and value investment principles. I will seek a healthy balance between risk and reward, aiming for controlled drawdowns while capturing upside potential. Diversification across sectors and moderate position sizing will be paramount.

## 1. Core Strategy: Blended Growth & Value with Opportunistic Momentum

My core strategy is a hybrid approach, combining the strengths of both growth and value investing, complemented by opportunistic momentum plays and informed by macro-economic analysis.

*   **Growth Component:** Identify companies with strong, sustainable revenue and earnings growth, expanding market share, innovative products/services, and robust competitive advantages. Focus on future potential and market leadership.
*   **Value Component:** Seek out fundamentally sound companies trading below their intrinsic value, characterized by strong balance sheets, consistent free cash flow generation, and attractive valuation multiples (e.g., P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA) relative to their industry peers and historical averages.
*   **Opportunistic Momentum:** While not a primary driver, I will leverage technical analysis to identify assets exhibiting strong, confirmed upward trends. These positions will be managed with tighter risk controls and a focus on trend continuation.
*   **Macro-Driven Context:** All investment decisions will be made within the context of the broader economic environment, considering factors like interest rates, inflation, and sector-specific tailwinds/headwinds.

## 2. Asset Selection: Diversified US Equities and ETFs

Asset selection will focus on high-quality US equities and strategic use of ETFs, ensuring broad diversification and adherence to my balanced mandate.

*   **Stock Picking Criteria:**
    *   **Fundamental Strength:** Prioritize companies with strong financial health, sustainable business models, competent management, and clear competitive advantages.
    *   **Growth Potential:** For growth stocks, look for accelerating revenue, EPS growth, and market expansion.
    *   **Value Discrepancy:** For value stocks, identify a clear catalyst for re-rating or a significant discount to intrinsic value.
    *   **Quality:** Regardless of growth or value, companies must demonstrate strong governance, ethical practices, and a clear path to profitability.
*   **Sector Allocation:** I will maintain a diversified portfolio across various sectors, avoiding overconcentration in any single industry. I will seek opportunities in sectors offering a mix of growth (e.g., Technology, Healthcare, Renewable Energy) and value (e.g., Industrials, Financials, Consumer Staples), adjusting exposure based on macro trends and relative valuations.
*   **ETFs Usage:** ETFs will be utilized for:
    *   **Broad Market Exposure:** To gain diversified exposure to specific market segments or themes (e.g., S&P 500, specific industries, factor-based ETFs).
    *   **Risk Management:** To quickly adjust overall market exposure or reduce single-stock risk.
    *   **Efficient Capital Deployment:** For rapid entry into or exit from broad market trends or sectors.
*   **Position Sizing:** Individual positions will typically range from **10% to 15%** of the total portfolio value, ensuring diversification and limiting the impact of any single underperforming asset. The maximum number of open positions will be 15.

## 3. Trade Horizon: Medium-Term (1-6 Months)

My typical trade horizon will be medium-term, generally ranging from **1 to 6 months**. This allows sufficient time for investment theses (growth realization, value recognition, trend continuation) to materialize, while also providing flexibility to adapt to changing market conditions. Positions will be held as long as the underlying thesis remains valid and the asset continues to perform in line with expectations.

## 4. Risk Profile: Controlled Drawdowns with Dynamic Management

My risk profile is designed to control drawdowns and protect capital, aligning with the target of under 15% portfolio drawdown.

*   **Typical Stop-Loss:** A mandatory stop-loss will be placed at **10% to 12%** below the entry price for individual positions. This allows for normal market volatility while ensuring prompt capital preservation if the investment thesis is invalidated or market conditions deteriorate.
*   **Typical Take-Profit:** Take-profit targets will generally aim for a **20% to 30%** gain, seeking a favorable risk-reward ratio of at least 2:1. However, profit-taking will be dynamic:
    *   For strong growth stocks, I may allow for greater upside, potentially using trailing stops to capture extended trends.
    *   For value plays, profit will be taken as the stock approaches its estimated intrinsic value.
    *   Positions will be re-evaluated regularly, and profits may be trimmed or taken entirely if the fundamental outlook changes, technical indicators signal weakness, or the risk-reward profile becomes unfavorable.

## 5. Data Usage: Fundamentals First, Macro Context, Technical Timing

My data usage will prioritize fundamental analysis for asset selection, contextualized by macro-economic data, and refined by technical analysis for optimal timing and risk management.

1.  **Fundamentals (Primary):** This is the cornerstone of my asset selection. I will heavily rely on financial statements, valuation metrics (P/E, P/S, EV/EBITDA, FCF yield), balance sheet health, competitive analysis, management quality, and industry trends to identify both growth and value opportunities.
2.  **FRED Macro (High Priority):** Macroeconomic data will provide the essential context for market conditions, sector rotation, and overall risk assessment. I will monitor key indicators such as GDP growth, inflation, interest rates, employment data, and consumer sentiment to inform my strategic allocation and risk posture.
3.  **Technicals (Secondary, for Timing & Risk):** Technical analysis will be used to confirm trends, identify optimal entry and exit points, set precise stop-loss levels, and manage positions. I will look at price action, volume, moving averages, and key support/resistance levels.
4.  **FINRA News (Supportive & Reactive):** News feeds will be monitored for company-specific catalysts, industry developments, earnings reports, and any events that could impact my investment theses. This tool is crucial for real-time adjustments and validating or invalidating existing positions.
