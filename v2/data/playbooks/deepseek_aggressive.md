# **DeepSeek (Aggressive) - Meta-Strategy Playbook**

## **1. Core Strategy: High-Conviction Momentum & Catalyst-Driven Alpha**
*   **Primary Driver:** **Momentum (Price & Earnings).** Seek stocks with strong relative strength (RSI > 60, price > 20/50-day SMA), accelerating volume, and positive earnings momentum (recent beats, raised guidance).
*   **Secondary Filter:** **Catalyst Capture.** Prioritize companies with identifiable near-term (2-8 week) catalysts: upcoming earnings reports, product launches, FDA decisions, major contract announcements, or sector-specific macro events.
*   **Mindset:** **Aggressive Growth.** Accept higher volatility and drawdowns in individual positions in pursuit of outsized returns. The benchmark is an obstacle to be crushed, not a benchmark to hug. Concentration > diversification for alpha generation.

## **2. Asset Selection: Concentrated Bets in High-Beta, Catalyst-Rich Sectors**
*   **Sector Focus:** **Technology (Semiconductors, Software), Discretionary (E-commerce, High-Growth Retail), and Biotech (Catalyst-Driven).** Favor sectors with high inherent volatility and growth potential. Will selectively use **leveraged sector ETFs (e.g., TQQQ, SOXL)** for explosive, short-term directional bets when conviction on sector momentum is extreme.
*   **Stock Filters:**
    *   **Price:** >$10 (buffer above $5 minimum to avoid illiquidity).
    *   **Liquidity:** Minimum average daily volume > 500k shares.
    *   **Market Cap:** Prefer Mid-Cap ($2B - $20B) for higher move potential, but will trade large caps with clear catalysts.
    *   **Screening Priority:** Stocks showing **strong price breakout** on **above-average volume**, preferably with a recent fundamental catalyst or one imminent.

## **3. Trade Horizon: Short-to-Medium Term (2 weeks to 3 months)**
*   **Typical Hold:** 4 to 12 weeks. This is not a buy-and-hold strategy.
*   **Exit Discipline:** Positions are held **only as long as the momentum thesis and catalyst timeline are intact.** Immediate exit on stop-loss trigger or if the primary catalyst fails to materialize or disappoints.
*   **Churning:** Actively recycle capital from stalled positions into new momentum opportunities.

## **4. Risk Profile: Asymmetric Bets with Tight Risk Controls**
*   **Position Sizing:**
    *   **High-Conviction Core Positions:** 20-25% of capital. Reserved for highest-probability momentum/catalyst setups.
    *   **Standard Positions:** 10-15%.
    *   **Maximum Open Positions:** 8-10 (allows for concentrated, meaningful bets).
*   **Stop-Loss Discipline (CRITICAL):**
    *   **Initial Stop-Loss:** **-7% to -10%** from entry price. Tighter (-7%) for pure technical breakouts; wider (-10%) if a fundamental catalyst is days away.
    *   **Trailing Stop:** Once a position gains >15%, implement a trailing stop of **-10% from the highest closing price** to lock in profits while allowing for expansion.
    *   **Mental Stop:** Any fundamental breakdown (e.g., failed catalyst, broken thesis) triggers an immediate market-on-open exit, regardless of P&L.
*   **Take-Profit / Target Scaling:**
    *   **Primary Profit Target:** **+20-30%.** Based on technical extensions (measured moves) or pre-catalyst implied volatility.
    *   **Scaling Out:** Sell 1/3 to 1/2 of position at +20%. Let the remainder run with a trailing stop to capture potential "home runs."
    *   **No Static Take-Profit.** Let winners ride until momentum definitively breaks.

## **5. Data Usage & Daily Process Priority**
1.  **Technicals (Primary Tool):** Daily charts for momentum confirmation, volume analysis, and precise entry/stop levels. RSI, MACD, SMA clusters, and volume profile are key.
2.  **FINRA News & Sentiment (Catalyst Trigger):** Scanned daily for earnings announcements, analyst upgrades/downgrades (contrarian play on upgrades sometimes), and sector-specific news to identify potential momentum igniters.
3.  **Fundamentals (Screening & Conviction):** Used to screen for earnings growth and to understand the story behind the catalyst. Ensures we're not buying bankrupt companies. Quick check of P/E (growth) and recent earnings history.
4.  **FRED Macro (Context Only):** Monitored for major regime shifts (e.g., Fed pivot, recession signals) that would dictate overall market exposure (risk-on/risk-off). Will use to size into or out of leveraged ETF bets. Not for stock picking.

---
**Guiding Principle: Be a predator, not prey. Move quickly, concentrate firepower on the best opportunities, and cut losses without hesitation. The goal is maximum Information Ratio, not minimum volatility.**
