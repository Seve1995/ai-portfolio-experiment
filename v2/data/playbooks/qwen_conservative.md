# Conservative Portfolio Manager Investment Playbook

## 1. Core Strategy
- **Capital Preservation**: The primary focus is on preserving capital, with a preference for established, large-cap companies and broad-market ETFs.
- **Value and Quality**: Emphasis on value investing, focusing on companies with strong fundamentals, consistent earnings, and a history of stability.
- **Macro-Driven Adjustments**: Incorporate macroeconomic data to adjust portfolio allocations, but with a conservative bias.

## 2. Asset Selection
- **Stocks**:
  - **Sectors**: Favor defensive sectors such as Consumer Staples, Healthcare, and Utilities.
  - **Criteria**:
    - Market Cap: Large-cap (>$10B)
    - Dividend Yield: Prefer companies with a stable dividend yield.
    - Earnings Stability: Consistent earnings growth over the past 5 years.
    - Debt-to-Equity Ratio: Below industry average.
    - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio: Below industry average.
- **ETFs**:
  - **Broad-Market ETFs**: Use ETFs like SPY (S&P 500), DIA (Dow Jones Industrial Average), and QQQ (Nasdaq 100) for broad market exposure.
  - **Sector ETFs**: Consider sector-specific ETFs in defensive sectors (e.g., XLP for Consumer Staples, XLV for Healthcare).

## 3. Trade Horizon
- **Holding Period**: Typically hold positions for 1-3 months, with a maximum of 6 months.
- **Rebalancing**: Rebalance the portfolio quarterly to maintain risk parameters and adjust for any macroeconomic changes.

## 4. Risk Profile
- **Position Sizing**: 
  - Initial position size: 5% to 10% of total capital.
  - Maximum of 15 open positions at any given time.
- **Stop-Loss**:
  - Set a wide stop-loss to avoid being stopped out by normal volatility.
  - Typical Stop-Loss: 10% below the entry price.
- **Take-Profit**:
  - Target a modest profit margin, aiming for a 10% to 15% gain.
  - Adjust take-profit levels based on market conditions and individual stock performance.

## 5. Data Usage
- **Prioritize Fundamentals**:
  - Financial statements, earnings reports, and company news.
  - Focus on key metrics such as P/E ratio, debt-to-equity ratio, and dividend yield.
- **Macroeconomic Data (FRED)**:
  - Use FRED data to monitor key economic indicators such as GDP, inflation, and interest rates.
  - Adjust portfolio allocations based on macroeconomic trends.
- **Technical Analysis**:
  - Use technicals to identify entry and exit points, but not as the primary decision-making tool.
  - Key indicators: Moving Averages (50-day and 200-day), Relative Strength Index (RSI), and Bollinger Bands.
- **News and Sentiment (FINRA News)**:
  - Monitor news for any significant events that could impact the portfolio.
  - Use sentiment analysis to gauge market mood and make tactical adjustments.

## Summary
- **Core Strategy**: Value and quality investing with a focus on capital preservation.
- **Asset Selection**: Defensive sectors and large-cap stocks, supplemented by broad-market and sector ETFs.
- **Trade Horizon**: 1-3 months, with quarterly rebalancing.
- **Risk Management**: Wide stop-losses (10%) and modest take-profits (10-15%).
- **Data Prioritization**: Fundamental analysis, macroeconomic data, and technicals for entry/exit points.

This playbook will guide all trading decisions to ensure a conservative and disciplined approach to managing the portfolio.
